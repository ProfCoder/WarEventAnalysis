When you hear Mainau, you think of flowers - that's what the third largest island in Lake Constance is known for and loved for. On an area of ​​around 45 hectares, visitors are transported to a Mediterranean oasis where enjoyment and relaxation are just as welcome as amazement and discovery. The island, which is around one kilometre long, is owned by the noble Bernadotte family from Sweden, who still reside in the castle complex today. And although some areas of this baroque building from the mid-18th century are open to the public, most guests come for a different reason: they want to marvel at the magnificent specimens that nature produces.

flower island in Lake Constance

For example, there is the park-like arboretum with its around 500 deciduous and coniferous trees, some of which are over 150 years old: In addition to precious Atlas and Lebanon cedars and impressive tulip trees, one of the oldest sequoias in Germany can be found northwest of the castle. If you are looking for Mediterranean flora and a tropical flair, the Palm House is the place for you: In addition to citrus plants, there are also over 20 types of palm trees - including a 15-meter-high Canary Island date palm that was planted on Mainau in 1888. The butterfly house is similarly exotic, where you might find a colorful butterfly from Africa, Asia, Central or South America landing right on your nose.

Mainau open all year round

A great advantage of the flower island: it fascinates its visitors all year round. In spring, the orchid show in the palm house with over 3,000 specimens is an unforgettable sight, as is the "sea" of tulips with almost 400 different varieties. In summer, the flowers in the Italian rose garden are in full bloom, and from the end of August, around 12,000 dahlias create a firework display of colors and shapes.

The island, which belongs to the city of Konstanz , is accessible 365 days a year - from sunrise to sunset. You can reach Mainau by public transport (bus, boat) as well as by bike or car (parking on the mainland). Dogs are allowed on the island of Mainau, on a leash, but not in the butterfly house or in the castle's exhibition rooms.
